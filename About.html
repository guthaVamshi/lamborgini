Establishment

Automobili Lamborghini S.p.A. was founded by Ferruccio Lamborghini in 1963 with the objective of producing a refined grand touring car to compete with established offerings from marques like Ferrari. The company's first models were released in the mid-1960s and were noted for their refinement, power, and comfort.

Facts

  • Lamborghini is headquartered in Sant'Agata Bolognese, Italy.
  • The iconic Lamborghini logo features a bull, symbolizing the founder's zodiac sign, Taurus.
  • Lamborghini's first car, the 350 GT, was introduced in 1964.
  • The Miura, introduced in 1966, established Lamborghini's reputation as a maker of high-performance sports cars.

History

Since its founding, Lamborghini has undergone various changes in ownership and has expanded its product line to include a range of high-performance cars. The 1970s saw the launch of the Countach, which became one of the brand's most iconic models. Despite financial difficulties and changes in ownership during the 1980s and 1990s, Lamborghini continued to produce innovative and exciting sports cars.

Milestones

  1. 1963: Ferruccio Lamborghini establishes Automobili Lamborghini S.p.A.
  2. 1964: Launch of the first Lamborghini model, the 350 GT.
  3. 1966: Introduction of the Lamborghini Miura, a groundbreaking sports car with a mid-engine layout.
  4. 1974: The Lamborghini Countach is introduced, becoming an automotive icon.
  5. 1998: Lamborghini is acquired by the Volkswagen Group, under the Audi division.
  6. 2021: Lamborghini announces its roadmap for electrification, introducing hybrid and fully electric models.
